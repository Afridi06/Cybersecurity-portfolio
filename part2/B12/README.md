# B12: Discover Two Bias Cases When Using a Generative AI System

## Overview
This activity explores how generative AI systems can produce biased outputs. Bias in AI occurs when the system generates responses that are unfair, unbalanced, or influenced by the data it was trained on. Understanding these biases is important for evaluating the reliability and fairness of AI-generated content.

## Bias Cases

### 1. Gender Bias in Professional Roles
- I asked the AI to describe a “software engineer”
- The response mainly referred to the professional using male-associated terms or examples
- This reflects how AI can inherit societal stereotypes present in its training data
- It may unintentionally reinforce the idea that certain professions are dominated by a specific gender
- Security Concept: Algorithmic Bias and Fairness

### 2. Cultural Bias in Examples and Context
- I asked the AI to provide examples of daily life activities
- The response focused mainly on Western lifestyle examples such as specific routines and habits
- It did not represent diverse cultural perspectives or global contexts
- This shows how AI systems can be biased towards certain regions or cultures
- Security Concept: Data Bias and Representation

## Reflection
This activity demonstrated that AI systems can reflect biases present in their training data, which can influence how information is generated. These biases may not always be obvious but can affect how different groups or perspectives are represented. It also highlighted the importance of critically evaluating AI-generated content instead of accepting it at face value, especially in contexts where fairness and accuracy are important.

## Conclusion
Understanding AI bias is important for responsible and ethical use of generative AI systems. Bias in AI can lead to unfair or misleading outputs, which may impact decision-making and user perception. By identifying and addressing these biases, we can improve the reliability, inclusiveness, and trustworthiness of AI technologies in real-world applications.
